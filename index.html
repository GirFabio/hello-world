The Impact of Twitter on Political Participation.

Abstract: Since Ellsberg, many studies have investigated the impact of model un- certainty on optimal portfolio allocations with a typical focus on ambiguity aversion. However, recent experimental evidence suggests that also ambi- guity prudence plays an important role in decision-making. In this work, we investigate the contribution of ambiguity prudence when investor eval- uates portfolio compositions according to the Smooth ambiguity criterion. We derive a third-order approximation of the certainty equivalent to dis- entangle prudence eects among beliefs and behavioural attitudes. Later on, we focus on two relevant examples: in the first, returns are i.i.d. with unknown expected return over which investor attaches skewed beliefs. In the second one, investor is ambiguous about excess-returns predictability. We show that, when the certainty equivalents implied by the models under evaluation are significantly different, ambiguity prudence induces a reduc- tion of stock allocation because of the investor's willingness to hedge against downside model uncertainty.
